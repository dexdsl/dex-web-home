Proces‌s & timeline

  1. Intake & quick screen (1–2 weeks) — COI check; 10–15 min pre-brief call; confirm seat fit, availability, geography.
  2. Closed-door briefing (1 week window) — mission, ops, CC-BY constraints; candidate selects path (Observer · Named Advisor · Committee preview).
  3. Preview / trial (30–60 days) — path task (FP&A slice · CC memo · major gifts list · arts-tech community plan · director-of-video brief). Two references; chair sign-off.
  4. Committee recommendation — dossier (seat, class, term) to board; conflicts cleared; D&O confirmed.
  5. Board vote & onboarding — agreement, COI, welcome kit; observer debrief if any.

What we as‌ses‌s

  • Mission alignment — open-access, library-first; comfort with CC-BY 4.0.
  • Governance — judgment, reliability (~6 hrs/mo), fiduciary awareness, confidentiality.
  • Seat skills — Legal/IP (CC), Finance/FP&A, Development (major gifts/university), Community/Arts-Tech, Director of Video.
  • Network leverage — major gifts, university gateways, arts/tech partners.
  • Geography — LA/FL/DC/Baltimore priority; remote welcome.

For nominators

You know who makes rooms smarter. If someone in your orbit lives the open-access ethos and can move an arts/tech nonprofit forward, we want to learn their work. Dex exists to advance artmaking by putting high-quality performance media in the commons (CC-BY 4.0) — free, forever. This season we’re building dexDRONES, hosting JHU/Peabody residencies, and producing Season 3 commissions and events.

Fit at a glance

  • Ethos: library-first, comfortable with Creative Commons / CC-BY 4.0.
  • Conflicts: COI applies — not employed by PROs (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC, GMR), major labels, streaming, or ad-tech.
  • Time: ~6 hours/month; board meets quarterly with focused work between.
  • Geography: LA / FL / DC / Baltimore preferred; remote welcome.

How it works

Search/Governance handles intake and briefings; the board appoints. Full process, timing, and paths (Director, Named Advisor, Observer) live in the Overview and Process tiles. If you’re weighing a nomination, start there to see expectations and boundaries. Then think about the person who will actually ship something meaningful for artists — that’s the person we hope you’ll put forward.
