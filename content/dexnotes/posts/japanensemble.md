  <p style="text-align:justify;white-space: normal !important;white-space:pre-wrap;" class="">Recently, the CalArts Herb Alpert Music School announced a change to its budget, cutting parts of the World Music department to make way for new offerings in the program. One of the affected programs was the Japanese ensemble, which came to us to see if we couldn’t find a way to fund the group for another year of performances, recordings, and Japanese-American Culture.</p><h4 style="white-space:pre-wrap;">What is it?</h4><p style="text-align:justify;white-space: normal !important;white-space:pre-wrap;" class="">The CalArts Japanese Ensemble, headed by Kozue Matsumoto (<em>Shōgun, </em>2024, FX/Hulu; <em>Ghost of Tsushima</em>, 2020, Sucker Punch Studios/Sony), is a consort made up of koto, shamisen, shakuhachi, and taiko players; students learn techniques, rehearse traditional and contemporary compositions, and obtain knowledge of cultural ethnology by partaking in this ensemble. Throughout the school year, they practice and perform throughout the city, sharing Japanese musical culture with the greater Los Angeles area.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Without funding, students now have to cover expenses like concert catering, instruments and instrument repairs, and travel costs.  </p>
